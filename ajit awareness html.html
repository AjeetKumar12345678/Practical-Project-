Introduction Of Alcohol Awareness

Alcoholism, excessive and repetitive drinking of alcoholic beverages to the extent that the drinker repeatedly is harmed or harms others.
The harm may be physical or mental; it may also be social, legal, or economic.
Because such use is usually considered to be compulsive and under markedly diminished
voluntary control, alcoholism is considered by a majority of, but not all, clinicians as an addiction and a disease.

  • Psychological well-being: Low to moderate alcohol consumption can improve mood, reduce depression, and increase feelings of happiness and euphoria.
  • Cognitive performance: Low doses of alcohol can improve short-term memory and problem-solving.
  • Heart health: Moderate drinking can reduce the risk of stroke, heart attack, and hardened arteries.
